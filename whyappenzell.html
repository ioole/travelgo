Reasons to visit Appenzell.

  • 1. One of the most famous hiker paradise.

    • During summer, the Alpstein is well known for being a hiker's paradise. Two popular destinations are Ebenalp and Hoher Kasten. They are not challenging but beautiful, so these hikes will not bore experienced hikers.

  • 2. Hof Weissbad.

    • Stay at the four-star Hof Weissbad, just minutes from the town of Appenzell, for a perfect getaway to Eastern Switzerland. A romantic lantern-lit trail through the nearby forest is just steps away from the hotel. Steam baths and Finnish saunas are available in the spa area.
    After a day of physical activity in the mountains, nothing beats a trip to the spa to relax those tense muscles. The highest standards are strictly adhered to, and the hotel is known for consistently winning awards for being one of Switzerland's best employers. This becomes clear when you consider the staff's friendliness and hospitality. When you consider the staff's friendliness and hospitality, it's easy to see why.

  • 3. Indulge in some finest local music

    • Appenzell string music, which includes hammered dulcimer, double bass, cello, and two violins, is well-known around the world. There is a possibility that the most famous string music ensemble are the Alder family from Urnäsch. They have performed string music together since 1884 and today the different line-ups of the group including 3rd-, 4th- and 5th-generation members of the family. There is also a good chance you will hear the dulcimer because it is an integral part of Appenzeller music in Appenzell.

  • 4. Säntis cable car

    • The railway line built to Wasserauen at the turn of the century was actually the first section of a planned railway up the Säntis. However, the railway line never extended beyond the valley town, so the cable car from Schwägalp to the top of the Säntis was inaugurated in 1935. From the valley station, you glide almost weightlessly up to the imposing summit in about 10 minutes. On clear days, the mountain offers breathtaking views of France, Germany, Italy, Austria, Liechtenstein, and Switzerland from 2,502 metres above sea level. During the summer, the mountain attracts visitors with a variety of special experiences such as full moon trips, sunrise ascents, and Säntis-Spitzegnoss evenings. It is also appropriate for people of all ages.

  • 5. Hoher Kasten, the Top of Appenzell

    • Appenzell's summit Providing Informative panorama boards and free telescopes with integrated peak identification that explain the surrounding environment. Discover more than 300 species of Alpstein plants on a stroll or guided tour through the summit's Alpine garden. A wheelchair-friendly panoramic trail with attractive viewing spots now circles the restaurant in a large arc. The newly created geologic hike also begins on the Hoher Kasten, with 17 boards explaining the shaping, folds, and fractures of the rocky terrain. A gondola-style rail connects Stauberenkanzel to the Rhine Valley, with a geological path leading further afield, via Saxerlücke, to the Fählensee lake, taking 3 hours and 30 minutes.
