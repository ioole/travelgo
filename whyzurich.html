Reason to visit Zurich

  • 1. Dolder Ice Complex

    • Atmosphere around Dolder Ice Complex is specially unique. Why? It is surrounded by wooded countryside, with too 6,000 square meter ice rink which is the biggest artificial ice rink in Switzerland. Forming a relaxing and quiet place for locals and visitors. The complex, which has been renovated and expanded in recent years, blends in seamlessly with the surroundings. Not to mention, other leisure facilities are provided such as a swimming pool (with wave-making equipment) and minigolf course close by the Dolder ice rink . This is the most popular meeting place for all sport fans at all ages.

  • 2. Kunsthaus Zürich

    • The area around Kunsthaus Zürich has become very dynamic again with the openings of many established, but also young and innovative galleries.

    💡 Charlotte von Stotzingen, Director of Zurich Art Weekend

    The Kunsthaus Zürich boasts largest collection of modern art in Zurich.

    It is well-known for its permanent art collection as well as its temporary exhibitions. It contains works by Alberto Giacometti as well as special pictures by Picasso, Monet, and Chagall, as well as numerous contemporary Swiss artists. The spectacular extension designed by David Chipperfield Architects will make the Kunsthaus Zürich Switzerland's largest art museum.

  • 3. Lindt Home of Chocolate

    • Every CHOCOLATE lover will love this place!

    A tour of the Lindt Home of Chocolate covers every section of Swiss cultural asset chocolate, including a chocolate fountain over 9m tall and the biggest Lindt Chocolate Shop in the world, spread over 500m2.
    In the CHOCOLATERIA, you can create your own masterpieces under the expert guidance of Lindt Master Chocolatiers while learning about the origin, history, and manufacture of Lindt chocolate.
    During your visit to the first Lindt café in Switzerland, you can also indulge in a variety of sweet and savoury treats. Enjoy a memorable chocolate adventure that will impress your customers, coworkers, business partners, or family.
    Start your event off right with exquisite catering from the Lindt Home of Chocolate Lounge.

  • 4. Zürich Zoo

    • More than 340 animal species live in near-natural habitats at the Zürichberg Zoo. Around 4,000 animals act as ambassadors for their wild counterparts. A nature conservation centre participates in international breeding programmes and resettlement projects. Ecosystems have been created here to ensure that animals, including endangered species, can roam freely across large areas, ranging from the Himalayas and South American grasslands to the Masoala Rainforest Hall and the Kaeng Krachan Elephant Park. The Zoo also allows for close encounters between visitors and native livestock as well as domesticated animals.

    What are you waiting for? Come on now!

  • 5. Zurich's Old Town

    • The most clubs in Switzerland, one of the most famous shopping streets, and a plethora of cultural offerings. And all of this takes place against a mediaeval background. The Old Town of Zurich is a social, cultural and historical main destionation.

    Zurich's Medieval houses, contorted, narrow lanes, and Renaissance guild and town halls provide an appealing backdrop for world-class entertainment. Special tour of Zurich's Old Town allows visitors to learn about the city's varied history. The backdrop becomes a living history of notable structures and the people who lived and acted out their lives in them. Zurich's landmark is the Grossmünster (Great Minster) with its twin towers. According to legend, Charlemagne built the towers on the site where the city saints Felix and Regula's graves were discovered. The Peterskirche (Peter's Church), which has Europe's largest clockface, and the Fraumünster are also worth seeing.
