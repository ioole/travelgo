Reason to visit Pilatus

  • 1. Ride the dragon to Mount Pilatus

    • Technically, this dragon does not breathe fire..but it is indeed hot. Why is that? The “Dragon Ride” speeded to Pilatus Kulm from Fräkmüntegg straight around over 2,132 m above sea level which is super high.

  • 2. Snowshoe hiking on Pilatus

    • Want some peace and nature time?. Pilatus offers some inner peace and nature bonding with snowshoe hiking! Three signposted snowshoe trails link Krienseregg and Fräkmüntegg. The routes for it are all considered easy for beginners, therefore families and newcomers tend to come here.

      a. Krienseregg-Gibelegg-Dorschnei-Krienseregg

    • This hike takes under 2 hours to complete. It also provides enourmous and glorious birds-eye view over the city of Lucerne and Lake Lucerne. Tired? Hungry? Have a delicious snack by stopping at Berghaus Dorschnei.

      • b. Fräkmüntegg-Bonere-Mülimäs-Gibelegg-Krienseregg

    • Snowshoe hikers can access the nearest cable car from Kriens to Fräkmüntegg. The hike to Krienseregg takes about an hour and a half to complete, with only one gentle ascent. Hikers can replenish their energy levels at by dropping off at Berghaus Rickenschwändi.

      • c. Fräkmüntegg-Bonern-Rehlöcher-Fräkmüntegg

    • This circular trail with moderate ascents can be easily completed in a single morning, making it suitable for the entire family. The route offers spectacular enormous views of Lucerne, Lake Lucerne, and the Pre-Alps.
      If our guests fancy some beverages, head into Restaurant Fräkmüntegg at the end of the trail for a hot chocolate or a cup of coffee.

  • 3. Fräkmüntegg adventures

    • Adrenaline junkie or connoisseur, young or old, the Fräkmüntegg adventures on Pilatus have something that will appeal to everyone.

      a. Dragon Alp

    • There are multiple adventures just waiting to be discovered all around Dragon Alp's large barbecue area. During lunchtime, Dragon Alp is the most ideal location for a barbecue party. The small kiosk too provides tasty sweet treats as something for those who are feeling a little bit peckish.

      • b. Treetop Walkway

    • This free treetop walkway allows the entire family to get closer, nearer with one touch to nature. Six of the 13 treetop nests provides information boards where visitors are able to learn more about the forest and the animals that live there.

      • c. Rope Park

    • Young adventurers and the young at heart looking for a special thrill will find it in the rope park, which requires no special training. The 11 varying difficulty courses, the monkey trees, and the Powerfan (freefall simulator) all can ensure tons of fun and action. Rope park beginners and aficionados alike will find plenty to enjoy, including balance and climbing challenges, cool ropeway descents, and difficult obstacles to overcome.

  • 4. Summer Toboggan Run upon Fräkmüntegg

    • Get into the swing of things on Switzerland's longest summer toboggan run. How long? 1350 metres. 1350 metres of tempo and top-notch fun. This thrilling ride down the 1350-meter-long gleaming steel channel begins on Fräkmüntegg (1415 metres altitude). It contains variety of cool curves, dragon caves, and uilt-in jumps that will make every toboggan run an unforgettable fun experience.
    The ride is opened in summer, in dry weather as well.
