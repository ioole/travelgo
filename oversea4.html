Japan

Osaka Castle is one of the most impressive castle buildings in Japan. The huge white building is a replica of the real castle from 1583 in the 1930s. Nishinomaru Garden is located in the building, which also has a modern museum and elevator for easy access. It is one of the most popular sakura viewing areas in the city.
Shitennoji Temple is one of the oldest temple ever in this worldConsider visiting one of Osaka's many shrines and temples after a stroll around the castle. A typical 12th-century Buddhist temple, Isshinji Temple, and Shitennoji, a Buddhist temple built in AD 593, are near the station. Not far away is Sumiyoshi Taisha, one of the oldest shrines in Japan, built in the 3rd century. There are four main halls and a beautiful inverted bridge.
Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan has Eight floors and have fifteen tanks, some of which span multiple floors. As you descend, you'll circle the massive central tank that houses an adult whale shark the size of a city bus. You may be ready to savour their flavour after a few hours of admiring the splendour of water life. A number of eateries, including those that specialise in squid and other seafood, are located immediately outside the aquarium
Dontori Area, Visitors can stop at the dotonbori area before returning to the hotel. This is one of the famous tourist attractions in Japan. Restaurants, amusement parks, and retail malls are open 24 hours a day, allowing visitors to sample the local nightlife. Here, you can stroll along the promenade of the Dotonbori River Canal, admiring brilliant advertisements and neon signs, including the iconic Glico Man.
Universal Studious Osaka is the second largest amusement park in Japan. There are a total of 8 areas with different themes. Among them, the most popular Harry Potter theme, followed by Jurassic Park, Amity Village, Hollywood, New York, Waterworld, Universal Wonderland and San Francisco. It is a shopping mall outside the park, it also has a specific theme and that's a movie theme. Many amusement attractions, ranging from kid-friendly carousels to terrifying roller coasters and simulators inspired on renowned films like Spiderman, Back to the Future, Terminator 2 and Jurassic Park, are available to visitors.
HEP Five Ferris Wheel is of the biggest wheel ever in this world. If you've ever wanted to ride a giant red Ferris wheel on top of a Japanese building, now is your chance. You have come to the right place. Hep Five is a 10-storey entertainment complex and shopping mall, in addition to an exciting experience. The 7th floor has many restaurants where local tourists like to gather and play, European and Japanese fashion stores and game centers. Finally, after a long and tiring trek, head to the top floor to ride the big red Ferris wheel, a romantic experience that many couples enjoy after sunset.
