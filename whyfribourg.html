Reason to visit Fribourg

  • 1. Musée H.R. Giger

    • The Saint-Germain castle, located in the centre of Gruyères' historic site, features one of the world's oddest museums.

    The Château Saint-Germain, located in the centre of Gruyères' historic site, holds one of the world's oddest museums. The exhibition, dedicated to the works of Swiss artist Hans Ruedi Giger (born 1940), transports viewers to a fascinating and terrifying science fiction environment. The artist's private collection is housed in a huge room. Known for his futuristic creations, the Graubünden artist and illustrator rose to prominence after taking part in the production of the film "Alien," for which he created the monster and won an Oscar in Hollywood in 1980.

  • 2. Grand-Places Park

    • The Jean Tinguely fountain stands out in this beautiful oasis in the heart of Fribourg. It was constructed in memory of his buddy and racing driver Jo Siffert by the current Fribourg artist.

    Jean Tinguely deserved the international acclaim he has achieved, and his works may be found in various European capitals and significant cities.

  • 3. Restaurant Plan Francey

    • Our wood-paneled business, which was rebuilt in spring 2017, provides hikers and skiers regional cuisine: we serve local Gruyere dishes, burgers, vegetarian dishes, and a variety of daily specialties at 1,520 metres above sea level.

  • 4. Urban Golf Downtown Fribourg

    • A 9-hole or 18-hole golf course that takes you through Fribourg's interesting old town. Combine athletics and entertainment while learning about the history of one of Switzerland's most stunning mediaeval towns.

    Fribourg's Urban Golf: an exciting activity similar to minigolf and swingolf, excellent for parties or families. The game is simple: point out, shoot, and tatlize the least shots with a 3-faced golf club, a ball, and a road map in your hand.

  • 5. Gorges du Gottéron

    • The Gottéron valley, whose dark side was so feared in the Middle Ages — legend had it that a dragon dwelt there – housed both blacksmiths and millers. It was located just outside of town, on the right bank, and was a perfect location for boisterous activities or those that required being close to a river without bothering the neighbours. It was in this valley that the Old Town's young people established Fribourg's first hockey club.

    The Place du Petit-Saint-Jean serves as the starting point for this circular trek. Cross the Bern bridge, which was built in 1250 and has been restored on a regular basis since then. Continue up the Rue des Forgerons, past the ramparts. The trail then follows the river into a wooded avine. If you're lucky, you might spot the chamois that have made their home on the molasses cliffs. Return to the Schürmatt farm, which stands on an open plateau, after visiting the historic Ameismühle mill. The path goes on through farmland and woodlands. In Bourguillon, use the cantonal road for a few hundred metres to the footpath beside the mediaeval tower. Cross the Bern bridge again at the Auberge de l'Ange to return to your starting point.

  • 6. Frÿburg 1606 - Discovery tour of mediaeval Fribourg

    • Fribourg's exceptional mediaeval splendour is attributed to the city's surviving walls and over 200 unique Gothic facades. The old town, like an open-air museum, is best explored on foot.

    The Cathedral of Fribourg, with its breathtaking stained-glass windows, is a sight to behold. It was dedicated to St. Nicholas and was built in phases beginning in 1283. Its 74-metre-high tower provides a breathtaking panoramic view. Fribourg's funiculaire is likewise well-known. The funicular, which connects the lower and upper parts of the city, is powered by waste water. This thriving academic town is a cosmopolitan micro-metropolis. Boutiques, antique shops, student cafes, and eateries providing regional specialties along the narrow lanes.
