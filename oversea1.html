Switzerland

Pilatus

Maker of weather, dragon's lair, home to giants and grave of rulers: Lucerne's very own mountain, Pilatus, is one of the most legendary hotspot in Central Switzerland. The most beautiful location as well. On a clear day, the mountain provides a 360-degree view of 73 Alpine peaks.

Zurich Region

Zurich is the must go location for leisure and pleasure. Gentle hills, tranquil woods, clean lakes and rivers, picturesque villages - all just a stone's throw from the Alps. Zurich is an excellent starting point for a wide range of excursions.

Appenzell

The village of Appenzell and the Canton of Appenzell Innerrhoden are specially located in a beautiful landscape of rolling hills. The region is famous for rural customs and traditions such as the ceremonial descent of the cattle in autumn and cultural events like folk music and rustic dances, not to mention hiking tours as well in the Alpstein region.

St. Moritz

St. Moritz is not just a holiday destination. Did you know that in 1864, St. Moritz was the birthplace of Alpine winter tourism and has twice hosted the Winter Olympics? St. Moritz rose to prominence as a result of its mineral springs, which were discovered 3,000 years ago and helped establish the town as a summer spa resort early on.
Click here to learn more.

Sörenberg

The Sörenberg holiday resort is located at an altitude of 1165 meters in the Entlebuch Unesco biosphere. This fantastic moor landscape, with its unique plant and animal life, earned the region the distinction of being one of only 400 biosphere reserves in the world. A true highlight for nature lovers and families.
Click here to learn more.

Fribourg Region

Winter sports, cultural and tradition as well as culinary delights, tons of unique stuff that are worth your visit. The Pays de Fribourg, a world turned upside down: cold, limestone summits are found in the south and mild, even Mediterranean-like lakesides in the north. On their small territory, two languages coexist and three very distinct holiday regions overlap: the Pre-Alps, the city of Fribourg and the Three-Lakes Region.
